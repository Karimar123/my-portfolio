Stages of a ransomware attack

A ransomware attack typically proceeds through the following stages.

Stage 1: Initial access

                                                The most common access vectors for ransomware attacks continue to be
                                                phishing and vulnerability exploitation.

Stage 2: Post-exploitation

                                                Depending on the initial access vector, this second stage may involve an 
                                                intermediary remote access tool (RAT) or malware prior to establishing 
                                                interactive access.

Stage 3: Understand and expand

                                                During this third stage of the attack, attackers focus on understanding the 
                                                local system and domain that they currently have access to, and on gaining 
                                                access to other systems and domains (called lateral movement).

Stage 4: Data collection and exfiltration

                                                Ransomware creators focus on finding valuable data and stealing it by downloading 
                                                or exporting it for themselves. They typically focus on material that can be used 
                                                for double extortion, such as intellectual property, login credentials, and customer 
                                                personal information.

Stage 5: Deployment and sending the note

                                                Crypto ransomware encrypts data and disables system restore functionality. 
                                                Non-encrypting ransomware locks the screen of the victim's device and inundates it 
                                                with pop-up ads. The ransomware notifies the victim of the infection once files have  
                                                been encrypted and/or the device has been deactivated, often by dropping a.txt file 
                                                on the desktop or by means of a pop-up notification. The ransom letter specifies 
                                                how to pay the ransom, typically through bitcoin or another similarly anonymous 
                                                mechanism.
